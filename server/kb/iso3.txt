

ISO 45001:2018 Occupational Health and Safety Management System

Overview
ISO 45001:2018 specifies requirements for an occupational health and safety (OH\&S) management system, and gives guidance for its use, to enable organizations to provide safe and healthy workplaces by preventing work-related injury and ill health, as well as by proactively improving its OH\&S performance.

Key Benefits

* Reduced work-related injuries and illnesses
* Lower insurance costs and reduced liability
* Improved regulatory compliance
* Enhanced employee morale and productivity
* Better reputation and stakeholder confidence
* Reduced absenteeism and employee turnover

High Level Structure (HLS)

Clause 4: Context of the Organization
4.1 Understanding the Organization and its Context
Determine external and internal issues relevant to purpose and that affect ability to achieve intended outcomes of OH\&S management system.

4.2 Understanding the Needs and Expectations of Interested Parties

* Workers and their representatives
* Regulatory authorities
* Parent organization and customers
* Healthcare providers and emergency services
* Neighboring communities
* Suppliers, contractors and visitors

4.3 Determining the Scope of the OH\&S Management System
Consider activities, products and services within organization's control or influence that can impact OH\&S performance.

4.4 OH\&S Management System
Establish, implement, maintain and continually improve OH\&S management system to achieve intended outcomes.

Clause 5: Leadership and Worker Participation
5.1 Leadership and Commitment
Top management must demonstrate leadership and commitment by:

* Taking overall responsibility and accountability for prevention of work-related injury and ill health
* Ensuring OH\&S policy and objectives are established and compatible with strategic direction
* Ensuring integration of OH\&S management system requirements into business processes
* Ensuring resources needed are available
* Eliminating hazards and reducing OH\&S risks

5.2 OH\&S Policy
OH\&S policy must:

* Be appropriate to purpose and context of organization
* Provide framework for setting OH\&S objectives
* Include commitment to provide safe and healthy working conditions
* Include commitment to eliminate hazards and reduce OH\&S risks
* Include commitment to fulfill legal requirements and other requirements
* Include commitment to continual improvement
* Include commitment to consultation and participation of workers

5.3 Organizational Roles, Responsibilities and Authorities
Assign responsibilities and authorities for:

* Ensuring OH\&S management system conforms to ISO 45001
* Reporting on performance of OH\&S management system to top management
* Ensuring workers can report hazards, hazardous situations and OH\&S incidents

5.4 Consultation and Participation of Workers
Establish, implement and maintain processes for consultation and participation of workers at all applicable levels and functions, and where they exist, workers' representatives.

Clause 6: Planning
6.1 Actions to Address Risks and Opportunities
6.1.1 General
When planning OH\&S management system, consider issues, requirements and determine risks and opportunities that need to be addressed.

6.1.2 Hazard Identification and Assessment of Risks and Opportunities
6.1.2.1 Hazard Identification
Establish, implement and maintain processes for hazard identification that:

* Is proactive and reactive
* Takes into account routine and non-routine activities and situations
* Takes into account human factors
* Takes into account how work is actually performed
* Takes into account past relevant incidents

6.1.2.2 Assessment of OH\&S Risks and Other Risks to the OH\&S Management System
Assess OH\&S risks from identified hazards, taking into account effectiveness of existing controls and other risks to OH\&S management system.

6.1.2.3 Assessment of OH\&S Opportunities and Other Opportunities for the OH\&S Management System
Assess OH\&S opportunities to enhance OH\&S performance and other opportunities for OH\&S management system.

6.1.3 Determination of Legal Requirements and Other Requirements
Determine and have access to up-to-date legal requirements and other requirements that are applicable to hazards, OH\&S risks and OH\&S management system.

6.1.4 Planning Action
Plan actions to address:

* Hazards and associated OH\&S risks
* Legal requirements and other requirements
* OH\&S risks and opportunities identified

6.2 OH\&S Objectives and Planning to Achieve Them
6.2.1 OH\&S Objectives
OH\&S objectives must:

* Be consistent with OH\&S policy
* Be measurable (if practicable) or capable of performance evaluation
* Take into account applicable requirements
* Take into account results of consultation with workers
* Be monitored, communicated and updated as appropriate

6.2.2 Planning to Achieve OH\&S Objectives
Determine what will be done, what resources required, who will be responsible, when it will be completed, and how results will be evaluated.

Clause 7: Support
7.1 Resources
Determine and provide resources needed for establishment, implementation, maintenance and continual improvement of OH\&S management system.

7.2 Competence

* Determine necessary competence of workers that affects or can affect OH\&S performance
* Ensure workers are competent based on appropriate education, training or experience
* Take actions to acquire and maintain necessary competence
* Evaluate effectiveness of actions taken

7.3 Awareness
Workers must be aware of:

* OH\&S policy and OH\&S objectives
* Their contribution to effectiveness of OH\&S management system
* Implications and potential consequences of not conforming
* Relevant OH\&S incidents and outcomes of investigations
* Hazards, OH\&S risks and actions that have been taken
* Ability to remove themselves from work situations they consider present imminent and serious danger

7.4 Communication
7.4.1 General
Establish, implement and maintain processes needed for internal and external communications relevant to OH\&S management system.

7.4.2 Internal Communication
Internally communicate information relevant to OH\&S management system between various levels and functions, including changes.

7.4.3 External Communication
Externally communicate information relevant to OH\&S management system as established by organization's communication processes and as required by legal requirements.

7.5 Documented Information
Standard requirements for creating, updating, and controlling documented information.

Clause 8: Operation
8.1 Operational Planning and Control
8.1.1 General
Plan, implement and control processes needed to meet OH\&S management system requirements and implement actions determined in Clause 6.

8.1.2 Eliminating Hazards and Reducing OH\&S Risks
Establish, implement and maintain processes to eliminate hazards and reduce OH\&S risks using hierarchy of controls:

1. Eliminate the hazard
2. Substitute with less hazardous processes, operations, materials or equipment
3. Use engineering controls and reorganization of work
4. Use administrative controls including training
5. Use adequate personal protective equipment

8.1.3 Management of Change
Establish processes to implement and control planned temporary and permanent changes that impact OH\&S performance.

8.1.4 Procurement
8.1.4.1 General
Establish, implement and maintain processes to control procurement of products and services to ensure conformity to OH\&S management system.

8.1.4.2 Contractors
Establish and implement processes to ensure contractors and their workers comply with organization's OH\&S management system requirements.

8.1.4.3 Outsourcing
Ensure outsourced functions and processes are controlled and coordinated with relevant external providers.

8.2 Emergency Preparedness and Response
Establish, implement and maintain processes needed to prepare for and respond to potential emergency situations.

Clause 9: Performance Evaluation
9.1 Monitoring, Measurement, Analysis and Performance Evaluation
9.1.1 General
Determine what needs to be monitored and measured, methods for monitoring, measurement, analysis and performance evaluation to ensure valid results.

9.1.2 Evaluation of Compliance
Establish, implement and maintain processes to evaluate compliance with legal requirements and other requirements.

9.2 Internal Audit
9.2.1 General
Conduct internal audits at planned intervals to provide information on whether OH\&S management system conforms to organization's requirements and ISO 45001 requirements, and is effectively implemented and maintained.

9.2.2 Internal Audit Programme
Plan, establish, implement and maintain audit programme including frequency, methods, responsibilities, consultation, planning requirements and reporting.

9.3 Management Review
Top management must review organization's OH\&S management system at planned intervals to ensure continuing suitability, adequacy and effectiveness.

Clause 10: Improvement
10.1 General
Determine opportunities for improvement and implement necessary actions to achieve intended outcomes of OH\&S management system.

10.2 Incident, Nonconformity and Corrective Action
10.2.1 General
Establish, implement and maintain processes to manage incidents and nonconformities including reporting, investigating and taking action.

10.2.2 Incident
When incident occurs:

* React in timely manner to incident and control and correct it
* Deal with consequences
* Evaluate with participation of workers and involvement of relevant interested parties, need for corrective action
* Implement corrective action including actions to eliminate root causes
* Evaluate effectiveness of corrective action taken
* Make changes to OH\&S management system if necessary

10.2.3 Nonconformity
When nonconformity occurs, react to it and take action to control and correct it, deal with consequences, and evaluate need for corrective action.

10.3 Continual Improvement
Continually improve suitability, adequacy and effectiveness of OH\&S management system to:

* Prevent occurrence of incidents and nonconformity
* Promote positive OH\&S culture
* Enhance OH\&S performance

Common Workplace Hazards
Physical Hazards

* Noise exposure
* Vibration (hand-arm, whole body)
* Temperature extremes (heat, cold)
* Radiation (ionizing, non-ionizing)
* Mechanical hazards (moving parts, sharp edges)
* Electrical hazards
* Compressed air and gases
* Working at height
* Confined spaces

Chemical Hazards

* Toxic substances
* Carcinogenic materials
* Corrosive chemicals
* Flammable and explosive materials
* Sensitizers and allergens
* Asbestos and other fibrous materials
* Dusts and particulates
* Gases and vapors

Biological Hazards

* Bacteria and viruses
* Fungi and molds
* Parasites
* Blood-borne pathogens
* Contaminated materials
* Waste materials
* Animals and insects
