

# ISO 50001:2018 – Energy Management System (EnMS)

 Overview

ISO 50001:2018 specifies requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and improving an energy management system (EnMS).
Its purpose is to help organizations:

 Improve energy performance (efficiency, use, and consumption)
 Reduce energy costs
 Decrease greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions and other environmental impacts
 Support sustainable development through systematic energy management

It applies to all organizations, regardless of size, type, or sector.

---

 Key Benefits

 Reduced energy consumption and operational costs
 Improved energy efficiency and sustainability
 Lower greenhouse gas emissions and environmental footprint
 Better compliance with national/international energy regulations
 Enhanced reputation with customers, regulators, and stakeholders
 Increased competitiveness through cost savings
 Encouragement of innovation in energy technologies and processes
 Structured framework for continuous improvement in energy performance

---

 High Level Structure (HLS)

 Clause 4: Context of the Organization

4.1 Understanding the Organization and Its Context
Identify external and internal issues affecting energy management objectives.

4.2 Understanding the Needs and Expectations of Interested Parties
Examples:

 Regulators and energy authorities
 Customers and investors
 Employees and management
 Local communities
 Energy suppliers and contractors

4.3 Determining the Scope of the EnMS
Define facilities, systems, and processes covered under the energy management system.

4.4 Energy Management System
Establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve the EnMS.

---

 Clause 5: Leadership

5.1 Leadership and Commitment
Top management must:

 Take accountability for energy performance and EnMS effectiveness
 Ensure energy policy and objectives are aligned with business strategy
 Provide resources to support energy management
 Promote continual improvement and energy-efficient culture

5.2 Energy Policy
The energy policy must:

 Commit to improving energy performance
 Support sustainable energy procurement and use of renewable energy
 Comply with applicable legal and regulatory requirements
 Be documented, communicated, and maintained

5.3 Organizational Roles, Responsibilities, and Authorities
Define roles for monitoring energy performance, reporting, and compliance.

---

 Clause 6: Planning

6.1 Actions to Address Risks and Opportunities
Identify energy-related risks and opportunities and integrate them into business planning.

6.2 Energy Review
A systematic analysis of:

 Current energy use and consumption
 Significant energy uses (SEUs)
 Factors affecting energy performance (equipment, processes, behavior, etc.)
 Opportunities for improvement

6.3 Energy Baseline (EnB)
Establish a reference baseline for measuring improvements in energy performance.

6.4 Energy Performance Indicators (EnPIs)
Select key indicators to measure and track energy performance (e.g., kWh per unit of output).

6.5 Objectives, Energy Targets, and Planning to Achieve Them
Define measurable objectives and action plans to improve energy performance.

---

 Clause 7: Support

7.1 Resources – Provide financial, technical, and human resources to maintain EnMS.
7.2 Competence – Ensure employees have the required skills for energy-efficient practices.
7.3 Awareness – Staff should understand:

 Energy policy and objectives
 Their roles in improving energy performance
 Consequences of inefficient energy practices

7.4 Communication

 Internal – Share performance updates across departments
 External – Communicate with regulators, suppliers, and stakeholders

7.5 Documented Information – Maintain records on energy performance, reviews, and targets.

---

 Clause 8: Operation

8.1 Operational Planning and Control
Plan and control operations that affect energy performance.

8.2 Design
Consider energy efficiency and performance when designing new facilities, equipment, or processes.

8.3 Procurement
Ensure purchased products and services impact energy performance positively (energy-efficient equipment, renewable energy sources).

---

 Clause 9: Performance Evaluation

9.1 Monitoring, Measurement, Analysis, and Evaluation
Track energy performance, EnPIs, and compare against baseline.

9.2 Internal Audit
Regular audits to check compliance with ISO 50001 and EnMS objectives.

9.3 Management Review
Top management must review energy performance and system effectiveness at planned intervals.

---

 Clause 10: Improvement

10.1 Nonconformity and Corrective Action – Address issues, identify causes, and take corrective steps.
10.2 Continual Improvement – Regularly enhance energy performance and system effectiveness.

---

 Common Areas of Energy Inefficiency (Risks)

 Equipment and Operations

 Old or poorly maintained machinery
 Inefficient HVAC (heating, ventilation, air conditioning) systems
 Poor insulation and building design
 Compressed air leaks

 Human and Process Factors

 Lack of employee awareness/training
 Inefficient operating practices (e.g., leaving machines running)
 Ineffective energy monitoring and reporting

 Energy Sources

 Over-dependence on fossil fuels
 Lack of renewable energy integration
 Inefficient procurement strategies
