

# ISO 22000:2018 – Food Safety Management System (FSMS)

 Overview

ISO 22000:2018 specifies requirements for a food safety management system that enables organizations in the food chain to:

 Consistently provide safe food products for consumption
 Prevent food-borne hazards and illnesses
 Demonstrate compliance with statutory, regulatory, and customer requirements
 Improve overall food safety performance

It applies to all organizations in the food chain, regardless of size or complexity – from primary producers to transporters, processors, retailers, and food-service outlets.

---

 Key Benefits

 Reduced risk of food-borne hazards and contamination
 Compliance with international food safety regulations and legal requirements
 Increased customer confidence and satisfaction
 Better integration of HACCP (Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points) into management system
 Reduced recalls, waste, and production losses
 Improved supplier and stakeholder relationships
 Stronger market access and competitive advantage
 Enhanced traceability and crisis management

---

 High Level Structure (HLS)

 Clause 4: Context of the Organization

4.1 Understanding the Organization and Its Context
Identify internal and external factors that impact food safety objectives.

4.2 Understanding the Needs and Expectations of Interested Parties
Interested parties may include:

 Customers and consumers
 Regulators and food safety authorities
 Suppliers and subcontractors
 Employees
 Food safety certification bodies
 Community and stakeholders

4.3 Determining the Scope of the FSMS
Define food chain activities, products, processes, and locations covered by FSMS.

4.4 Food Safety Management System
Establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve FSMS.

---

 Clause 5: Leadership

5.1 Leadership and Commitment
Top management must:

 Take responsibility for FSMS effectiveness
 Ensure food safety policy and objectives are established
 Provide necessary resources
 Promote continual improvement and food safety culture

5.2 Food Safety Policy
Policy must:

 Be appropriate to organization’s purpose and context
 Provide safe food for consumption
 Comply with applicable laws and regulations
 Support continual improvement
 Be communicated, understood, and maintained

5.3 Organizational Roles, Responsibilities, and Authorities
Clearly define roles for food safety, hazard control, monitoring, and reporting.

---

 Clause 6: Planning

6.1 Actions to Address Risks and Opportunities
Identify and control food safety risks and opportunities.

6.2 Food Safety Objectives and Planning
Objectives must be measurable, consistent with policy, and monitored.

6.3 Planning of Changes
Ensure changes do not negatively affect food safety.

---

 Clause 7: Support

7.1 Resources – Provide sufficient personnel, infrastructure, and technology for FSMS.
7.2 Competence – Ensure workers are trained in hygiene, hazard control, and food safety practices.
7.3 Awareness – Employees must be aware of:

 Food safety policy and objectives
 Hazards and control points
 Consequences of unsafe practices

7.4 Communication

 Internal – Between staff, departments, and management
 External – With suppliers, customers, regulators, and stakeholders

7.5 Documented Information – Maintain HACCP plans, monitoring records, traceability documents, and recall procedures.

---

 Clause 8: Operation

8.1 Operational Planning and Control
Implement processes needed to provide safe food.

8.2 Prerequisite Programmes (PRPs)
Basic hygiene and operational conditions (ISO/TS 22002 series) – e.g.:

 Cleaning and sanitation
 Pest control
 Personal hygiene
 Preventive maintenance
 Waste management

8.3 Traceability System
Ability to trace product batches from suppliers through production to customers.

8.4 Emergency Preparedness and Response
Plans for handling food safety emergencies (e.g., contamination, recalls).

8.5 Hazard Control (HACCP Principles)

 Hazard analysis (biological, chemical, physical hazards)
 Establish Critical Control Points (CCPs)
 Define critical limits for each CCP
 Monitoring procedures
 Corrective actions
 Verification procedures
 Documentation and record keeping

8.6 Control of Externally Provided Processes, Products, and Services
Ensure suppliers meet food safety requirements.

8.7 Control of Nonconforming Products and Processes
Prevent unsafe food from reaching consumers (withdrawal/recall system).

8.8 Verification Activities
Regular testing, monitoring, and auditing of FSMS.

---

 Clause 9: Performance Evaluation

9.1 Monitoring, Measurement, Analysis, and Evaluation – Define KPIs for food safety.
9.2 Internal Audit – Regular audits to verify compliance with ISO 22000 and HACCP.
9.3 Management Review – Top management reviews FSMS for adequacy and effectiveness.

---

 Clause 10: Improvement

10.1 Nonconformity and Corrective Action – Investigate incidents, take corrective actions, and prevent recurrence.
10.2 Continual Improvement – Enhance FSMS performance continuously.
10.3 Update FSMS – Reflect new technologies, regulations, and food safety trends.

---

 Common Food Safety Hazards

 Biological Hazards

 Bacteria (Salmonella, Listeria, E. coli, Campylobacter)
 Viruses (Norovirus, Hepatitis A)
 Parasites (Giardia, Trichinella)
 Molds, yeasts, fungi
 Spoilage organisms

 Chemical Hazards

 Pesticide residues
 Heavy metals (lead, mercury)
 Mycotoxins (aflatoxins)
 Food additives in excess
 Cleaning agent residues
 Allergens (gluten, nuts, dairy, soy, shellfish)

 Physical Hazards

 Glass, metal fragments, stones
 Plastic pieces
 Bone fragments
 Packaging material contamination
