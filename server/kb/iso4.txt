ISO 45001:2018 Occupational Health and Safety Management System

Overview
ISO 45001:2018 is the international standard for occupational health and safety (OH&S) management systems. It provides a framework for organizations to manage OH&S risks and improve OH&S performance by preventing work-related injury and ill health.

Key Benefits
- Reduced work-related injuries and illnesses
- Lower insurance costs and reduced liability
- Improved regulatory compliance
- Enhanced employee morale and productivity
- Better reputation and stakeholder confidence
- Reduced absenteeism and employee turnover
- Proactive risk management approach
- Integration with other management systems
- Global recognition and credibility

High Level Structure (HLS) - Detailed Breakdown

 Clause 4: Context of the Organization

4.1 Understanding the Organization and its Context
Organizations must identify external and internal issues that are relevant to their purpose and strategic direction and that affect their ability to achieve the intended outcomes of their OH&S management system.

**External factors may include:**
- Legal and regulatory environment
- Economic conditions
- Competitive environment
- Technological developments
- Social and cultural environment

**Internal factors may include:**
- Organizational structure
- Resources and capabilities
- Information systems
- Standards and models adopted
- Corporate culture and values

4.2 Understanding the Needs and Expectations of Interested Parties
Key interested parties include:
- Workers and their representatives
- Regulatory authorities and enforcement agencies
- Parent organizations and customers
- Healthcare providers and emergency services
- Neighboring communities
- Suppliers, contractors and visitors
- Insurance companies
- Trade unions
- Non-governmental organizations

4.3 Determining the Scope of the OH&S Management System
The scope must consider:
- Activities, products and services within the organization's control or influence
- Physical locations and boundaries
- Temporary and permanent activities
- External factors and interested party requirements
- Planned or new developments

4.4 OH&S Management System
Establish, implement, maintain and continually improve an OH&S management system in accordance with the requirements of ISO 45001.

 Clause 5: Leadership and Worker Participation

5.1 Leadership and Commitment
Top management must demonstrate leadership and commitment by:
- Taking overall responsibility and accountability for prevention of work-related injury and ill health
- Ensuring OH&S policy and objectives are established and compatible with strategic direction
- Ensuring integration of OH&S management system requirements into business processes
- Ensuring resources needed are available
- Communicating the importance of effective OH&S management
- Ensuring OH&S management system achieves its intended outcomes
- Directing and supporting workers to contribute to effectiveness
- Promoting continual improvement
- Supporting other relevant management roles

5.2 OH&S Policy
The OH&S policy must:
- Be appropriate to the purpose and context of the organization
- Provide a framework for setting OH&S objectives
- Include a commitment to provide safe and healthy working conditions
- Include a commitment to eliminate hazards and reduce OH&S risks
- Include a commitment to fulfill legal requirements and other requirements
- Include a commitment to continual improvement of the OH&S management system
- Include a commitment to consultation and participation of workers
- Be documented and communicated to workers
- Be available to interested parties
- Be reviewed periodically for continuing suitability

5.3 Organizational Roles, Responsibilities and Authorities
Top management must assign responsibility and authority for:
- Ensuring the OH&S management system conforms to the requirements of ISO 45001
- Reporting on the performance of the OH&S management system to top management
- Ensuring the integrity of the OH&S management system is maintained when changes are planned and implemented

5.4 Consultation and Participation of Workers
Organizations must establish processes for:
- Consultation on determining needs and expectations of interested parties
- Consultation on establishing OH&S policy
- Consultation on assigning organizational roles, responsibilities and authorities
- Consultation on determining how to fulfill legal requirements and other requirements
- Consultation on establishing OH&S objectives and planning to achieve them
- Consultation on determining applicable controls for outsourcing, procurement and contractors
- Consultation on determining what needs to be monitored, measured and evaluated
- Consultation on planning, establishing, implementing and maintaining audit programme
- Consultation on ensuring continual improvement

 Clause 6: Planning

6.1 Actions to Address Risks and Opportunities

6.1.1 General
When planning the OH&S management system, the organization must consider the issues referred to in 4.1, the requirements referred to in 4.2 and 4.3 and determine the risks and opportunities that need to be addressed.

6.1.2 Hazard Identification and Assessment of Risks and Opportunities

**6.1.2.1 Hazard Identification**
The organization must establish, implement and maintain processes for hazard identification that is ongoing and proactive and includes:
- Routine and non-routine activities and situations
- Human factors
- How work is actually performed
- Past relevant incidents, internal or external
- Potential emergency situations
- People who have access to the workplace
- Changes in knowledge and information about hazards

**6.1.2.2 Assessment of OH&S Risks and Other Risks**
The organization must establish, implement and maintain processes to:
- Assess OH&S risks from the identified hazards
- Take into account the effectiveness of existing controls
- Assess other risks to the OH&S management system

**6.1.2.3 Assessment of OH&S Opportunities and Other Opportunities**
The organization must establish, implement and maintain processes to assess:
- OH&S opportunities for enhancing OH&S performance
- Other opportunities for the OH&S management system

6.1.3 Determination of Legal Requirements and Other Requirements
The organization must determine and have access to the up-to-date legal requirements and other requirements that are applicable to its hazards, OH&S risks and OH&S management system.

6.1.4 Planning Action
The organization must plan:
- Actions to address these risks and opportunities
- How to integrate and implement the actions into its OH&S management system processes
- How to evaluate the effectiveness of these actions

6.2 OH&S Objectives and Planning to Achieve Them

6.2.1 OH&S Objectives
The organization must establish OH&S objectives at relevant functions and levels that:
- Are consistent with the OH&S policy
- Are measurable (if practicable) or capable of performance evaluation
- Take into account applicable requirements
- Take into account the results of assessment of risks and opportunities
- Take into account the results of consultation with workers
- Are monitored, communicated and updated as appropriate

6.2.2 Planning to Achieve OH&S Objectives
When planning how to achieve its OH&S objectives, the organization must determine:
- What will be done
- What resources will be required
- Who will be responsible
- When it will be completed
- How the results will be evaluated

 Clause 7: Support

7.1 Resources
The organization must determine and provide the resources needed for the establishment, implementation, maintenance and continual improvement of the OH&S management system.

7.2 Competence
The organization must:
- Determine the necessary competence of worker(s) that affects or can affect its OH&S performance
- Ensure that these workers are competent on the basis of appropriate education, training or experience
- Where applicable, take actions to acquire and maintain the necessary competence
- Evaluate the effectiveness of the actions taken
- Retain appropriate documented information as evidence of competence

7.3 Awareness
Workers must be aware of:
- The OH&S policy and OH&S objectives
- Their contribution to the effectiveness of the OH&S management system
- The implications and potential consequences of not conforming with the OH&S management system requirements
- Relevant OH&S incidents, and the outcomes of investigations that are relevant to them
- Hazards, OH&S risks and determined actions that are relevant to them
- The ability to remove themselves from work situations that they consider present an imminent and serious danger to their life or health

7.4 Communication

7.4.1 General
The organization must establish, implement and maintain the processes needed for internal and external communications relevant to the OH&S management system.

7.4.2 Internal Communication
The organization must internally communicate information relevant to the OH&S management system between the various levels and functions of the organization.

7.4.3 External Communication
The organization must externally communicate information relevant to the OH&S management system.

7.5 Documented Information

7.5.1 General
The organization's OH&S management system must include documented information required by ISO 45001 and documented information determined by the organization as being necessary for the effectiveness of the OH&S management system.

7.5.2 Creating and Updating
When creating and updating documented information, the organization must ensure appropriate identification, format, and review and approval for suitability and adequacy.

7.5.3 Control of Documented Information
Documented information must be controlled to ensure it is available and suitable for use, where and when it is needed, and is adequately protected.

 Clause 8: Operation

8.1 Operational Planning and Control

8.1.1 General
The organization must plan, implement and control the processes needed to meet OH&S management system requirements and to implement the actions determined in Clause 6.

8.1.2 Eliminating Hazards and Reducing OH&S Risks
The organization must establish, implement and maintain a process(es) for the elimination of hazards and the reduction of OH&S risks using the following hierarchy of controls:
1. Eliminate the hazard
2. Substitute with less hazardous processes, operations, materials or equipment
3. Use engineering controls and reorganization of work
4. Use administrative controls, including training
5. Use adequate personal protective equipment

8.1.3 Management of Change
The organization must establish a process(es) for the implementation and control of planned temporary and permanent changes that impact OH&S performance.

8.1.4 Procurement

**8.1.4.1 General**
The organization must establish, implement and maintain process(es) to control the procurement of products and services so as to ensure their conformity to its OH&S management system.

**8.1.4.2 Contractors**
The organization must establish and implement processes to ensure that contractors and their workers comply with the organization's OH&S management system requirements.

**8.1.4.3 Outsourcing**
The organization must ensure that outsourced functions and processes that impact the OH&S management system are controlled.

8.2 Emergency Preparedness and Response
The organization must establish, implement and maintain process(es) needed to prepare for and respond to potential emergency situations.

 Clause 9: Performance Evaluation

9.1 Monitoring, Measurement, Analysis and Performance Evaluation

9.1.1 General
The organization must determine what needs to be monitored and measured, the methods for monitoring, measurement, analysis and performance evaluation, and when the monitoring and measuring shall be performed and when the results shall be analyzed and evaluated.

9.1.2 Evaluation of Compliance
The organization must establish, implement and maintain process(es) to evaluate compliance with legal requirements and other requirements.

9.2 Internal Audit

9.2.1 General
The organization must conduct internal audits at planned intervals to provide information on whether the OH&S management system conforms to the organization's own requirements and to the requirements of ISO 45001, and is effectively implemented and maintained.

9.2.2 Internal Audit Programme
The organization must plan, establish, implement and maintain an audit programme(s).

9.3 Management Review
Top management must review the organization's OH&S management system, at planned intervals, to ensure its continuing suitability, adequacy and effectiveness.

 Clause 10: Improvement

10.1 General
The organization must determine opportunities for improvement and implement necessary actions to achieve intended outcomes of its OH&S management system.

10.2 Incident, Nonconformity and Corrective Action

10.2.1 General
The organization must establish, implement and maintain process(es) to manage incidents and nonconformities.

10.2.2 Incident
When an incident occurs, the organization must:
- React in a timely manner to the incident and, as applicable, control and correct it and deal with the consequences
- Evaluate, with the participation of workers and involvement of other relevant interested parties, the need for corrective action to eliminate the root cause(s) of the incident
- Implement any corrective action needed
- Evaluate the effectiveness of any corrective action taken
- Make changes to the OH&S management system, if necessary

10.2.3 Nonconformity
When a nonconformity occurs, the organization must react to the nonconformity and evaluate the need for action to eliminate the cause(s) of the nonconformity.

10.3 Continual Improvement
The organization must continually improve the suitability, adequacy and effectiveness of the OH&S management system to:
- Prevent the occurrence of incidents and nonconformity
- Promote a positive OH&S culture
- Enhance OH&S performance

Common Workplace Hazards

 Physical Hazards
- Noise exposure above safe levels
- Vibration (hand-arm, whole body)
- Temperature extremes (heat stress, cold exposure)
- Radiation (ionizing, non-ionizing)
- Mechanical hazards (moving machinery parts, sharp edges, crushing points)
- Electrical hazards (shock, burns, arc flash)
- Compressed air and gases
- Working at height (falls, falling objects)
- Confined spaces (atmospheric hazards, engulfment)
- Poor lighting conditions
- Inadequate ventilation
- Slips, trips and falls

 Chemical Hazards
- Toxic substances (acute and chronic effects)
- Carcinogenic materials
- Corrosive chemicals (acids, bases)
- Flammable and explosive materials
- Sensitizers and allergens
- Asbestos and other fibrous materials
- Dusts and particulates (silica, metal dusts)
- Gases and vapors (carbon monoxide, hydrogen sulfide)
- Skin and eye irritants
- Respiratory sensitizers

 Biological Hazards
- Bacteria and viruses (workplace-acquired infections)
- Fungi and molds (respiratory issues, allergic reactions)
- Parasites
- Blood-borne pathogens (hepatitis, HIV)
- Contaminated materials and surfaces
- Medical and clinical waste materials
- Animals and insects (bites, stings, disease transmission)
- Sewage and wastewater
- Food contamination

 Ergonomic Hazards
- Manual handling and lifting
- Repetitive motions
- Awkward postures
- Prolonged sitting or standing
- Excessive force requirements
- Poor workstation design
- Inadequate tool design

 Psychosocial Hazards
- Workplace stress and pressure
- Violence and harassment
- Bullying and discrimination
- Work-life balance issues
- Job insecurity
- Lack of control over work
- Poor communication
- Shift work and long hours

Implementation Tips

 Getting Started
1. Obtain top management commitment
2. Establish a project team
3. Conduct gap analysis against current practices
4. Develop implementation plan with timelines
5. Provide training and awareness programs
6. Begin with pilot areas or processes

 Key Success Factors
- Strong leadership commitment
- Active worker participation
- Adequate resource allocation
- Regular communication and feedback
- Integration with business processes
- Continuous monitoring and improvement
- Professional guidance and support

 Common Implementation Challenges
- Lack of management commitment
- Insufficient resources
- Resistance to change
- Poor communication
- Inadequate training
- Complex organizational structure
- Competing priorities
Certification Process

Steps to Certification
1. Gap analysis and planning
2. Implementation of OH&S management system
3. Internal audits
4. Management review
5. Selection of certification body
6. Stage 1 audit (documentation review)
7. Stage 2 audit (implementation assessment)
8. Certification decision
9. Surveillance audits (annual)
10. Recertification audit (every 3 years)

 Maintenance Requirements
- Continuous operation of OH&S management system
- Internal audits
- Management reviews
- Corrective actions for nonconformities
- Continual improvement initiatives
- Annual surveillance audits by certification body

ISO 45001:2018 provides organizations with a robust framework for managing occupational health and safety risks, protecting workers, and demonstrating commitment to workplace safety and health excellence.
