

# ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Information Security Management System (ISMS)

 Overview

ISO/IEC 27001:2022 specifies requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an information security management system (ISMS).
The objective is to help organizations protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information by applying a risk management process and giving confidence to interested parties.

---

 Key Benefits

 Enhanced protection of sensitive data (confidentiality, integrity, availability)
 Reduced risk of data breaches, cyber-attacks, and information loss
 Improved compliance with legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA)
 Lower financial losses due to security incidents
 Increased trust and confidence from customers, partners, and stakeholders
 Structured and systematic approach to managing information security risks
 Better incident response and business continuity
 Competitive advantage and improved reputation

---

 High Level Structure (HLS)
 Clause 4: Context of the Organization

4.1 Understanding the Organization and Its Context
Identify internal and external issues relevant to the ISMS objectives and outcomes.

4.2 Understanding the Needs and Expectations of Interested Parties
Interested parties may include:

 Customers and clients
 Employees and contractors
 Regulators and authorities
 Business partners and suppliers
 Shareholders and investors

4.3 Determining the Scope of the ISMS
Define the boundaries and applicability of the ISMS considering activities, processes, systems, and locations.

4.4 Information Security Management System
Establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve the ISMS.

---
 Clause 5: Leadership

5.1 Leadership and Commitment
Top management must:

 Take accountability for ISMS effectiveness
 Ensure policy and objectives align with business strategy
 Provide necessary resources
 Promote continual improvement

5.2 Information Security Policy
The policy must:

 Be appropriate to organization’s context
 Support objectives
 Commit to meeting requirements and continuous improvement
 Be communicated and available

5.3 Organizational Roles, Responsibilities, and Authorities
Clearly assign roles for ISMS management, incident handling, and reporting.

---
 Clause 6: Planning

6.1 Actions to Address Risks and Opportunities
Identify risks, opportunities, and legal requirements affecting ISMS.

6.1.2 Information Security Risk Assessment
Establish a process to:

 Identify risks to confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information
 Analyze and evaluate risks based on likelihood and impact
 Document results

6.1.3 Information Security Risk Treatment
Decide on risk treatment options:

 Risk acceptance
 Risk avoidance
 Risk transfer (e.g., insurance, outsourcing)
 Risk mitigation (controls from Annex A)

6.2 Information Security Objectives and Planning to Achieve Them
Objectives must be measurable, aligned with policy, monitored, and updated.

---
 Clause 7: Support

7.1 Resources – Provide adequate financial, technical, and human resources.
7.2 Competence – Ensure staff competence through training and awareness.
7.3 Awareness – Workers must be aware of ISMS policy, roles, risks, and reporting methods.
7.4 Communication – Define internal and external communication processes.
7.5 Documented Information – Requirements for creation, control, and retention of ISMS documents.

---
 Clause 8: Operation

8.1 Operational Planning and Control – Implement processes to achieve ISMS requirements.
8.2 Information Security Risk Assessment – Perform at planned intervals or when significant changes occur.
8.3 Information Security Risk Treatment – Implement selected controls and keep treatment plans updated.

---

 Clause 9: Performance Evaluation

9.1 Monitoring, Measurement, Analysis, and Evaluation – Define what to measure, methods, and frequency.
9.2 Internal Audit – Conduct audits to check ISMS conformity and effectiveness.
9.3 Management Review – Top management reviews ISMS at planned intervals.

---

 Clause 10: Improvement

10.1 Nonconformity and Corrective Action – Address incidents, identify root cause, and implement corrective action.
10.2 Continual Improvement – Enhance ISMS performance regularly.

---

 Annex A: Reference Controls (ISO 27002 aligned, 93 controls grouped into 4 themes)

1. Organizational Controls – Policies, roles, supplier relationships, compliance, business continuity.
2. People Controls – Background checks, training, responsibilities, awareness.
3. Physical Controls – Secure areas, equipment security, physical entry controls.
4. Technological Controls – Access control, cryptography, backup, logging, monitoring, malware protection, secure system development.

---

 Common Information Security Threats

 Technical Threats

 Malware (viruses, ransomware, spyware)
 Phishing and social engineering attacks
 Hacking and unauthorized access
 Denial of Service (DoS/DDoS) attacks
 Weak passwords and credential theft
 Misconfigured systems and unpatched vulnerabilities

 Physical Threats

 Unauthorized physical access to servers or data centers
 Theft of devices (laptops, USB drives, mobile phones)
 Natural disasters (fire, flood, earthquake)
 Power outages and hardware failures
 Human Factors

 Human error (accidental deletion, misconfiguration)
 Insider threats (malicious or negligent employees)
 Lack of awareness or training
 Social engineering exploitation
