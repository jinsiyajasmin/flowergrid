


# ISO 31000:2018 – Risk Management Guidelines

 Overview

ISO 31000:2018 provides principles, framework, and processes for risk management.
Unlike other ISO standards (which are certifiable, like 27001 or 22301), ISO 31000 is a guideline — it’s not for certification but gives organizations a structured approach to manage risk.

It helps organizations:

 Identify, assess, and treat risks effectively
 Improve decision-making and planning
 Increase resilience and adaptability
 Build stakeholder confidence

Applicable to organizations of any size, industry, or sector.

---

 Key Benefits

 Better decision-making by integrating risk into planning and operations
 Reduced losses from unexpected events and disruptions
 Improved governance, accountability, and resilience
 Enhanced stakeholder and investor confidence
 Increased ability to identify opportunities and not just threats
 Compliance with legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements
 Creation of a proactive risk-aware culture

---

 Principles of Risk Management (Core of ISO 31000)

ISO 31000 defines 8 principles that make risk management effective:

1. Integrated – Part of all organizational activities.
2. Structured and Comprehensive – Consistent, systematic approach.
3. Customized – Tailored to organization’s context and objectives.
4. Inclusive – Involving stakeholders in decision-making.
5. Dynamic – Adapts to changes in internal and external environment.
6. Best Available Information – Based on reliable and timely data.
7. Human and Cultural Factors – Recognizes people’s influence on risk.
8. Continual Improvement – Learning and adapting over time.

---

 Framework for Risk Management

 Clause 4: Framework

The framework ensures risk management is embedded across the organization.

Key elements include:

 Leadership and commitment from top management
 Integration into governance, strategy, and operations
 Resources and responsibilities defined
 Communication and reporting processes
 Continual improvement

---

 Risk Management Process

The ISO 31000 process is cyclical and iterative:

1. Communication and Consultation
Engage stakeholders at all levels to understand risks and decisions.

2. Scope, Context, and Criteria
Define the purpose, objectives, and boundaries of risk management.

3. Risk Assessment

 Risk Identification – Recognize events, hazards, or uncertainties that could affect objectives.
 Risk Analysis – Understand causes, sources, likelihood, and impact.
 Risk Evaluation – Compare risks against criteria to prioritize.

4. Risk Treatment
Select options to address risks:

 Avoid the risk (stop activity)
 Reduce the risk (controls, preventive measures)
 Share/transfer the risk (insurance, outsourcing, partnerships)
 Accept the risk (informed decision if within tolerance)

5. Monitoring and Review
Regularly check performance, changes, and effectiveness of risk treatment.

6. Recording and Reporting
Document results and share with decision-makers and stakeholders.

 Common Types of Risks Managed with ISO 31000

 Strategic Risks

 Market changes, competition, political instability
 Reputational damage
 Poor strategic decisions

 Operational Risks

 Process breakdowns, supply chain failures
 Equipment and system failures
 Workforce issues

 Financial Risks

 Credit risk, liquidity risk
 Currency exchange fluctuations
 Investment losses

 Compliance Risks

 Regulatory non-compliance
 Legal liabilities
 Data protection and privacy issues

 External Risks

 Natural disasters, pandemics
 Terrorism, war, or civil unrest
 Climate change impacts
