

# ISO 22301:2019 – Business Continuity Management System (BCMS)

 Overview

ISO 22301:2019 specifies requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving a business continuity management system (BCMS).

The purpose is to help organizations:

 Prepare for disruptive incidents (natural disasters, cyber-attacks, supply chain failures, pandemics, etc.)
 Minimize impact on operations when disruptions occur
 Ensure critical business functions continue or are quickly restored
 Build resilience and stakeholder trust

It applies to organizations of all types and sizes, across all sectors.

---

 Key Benefits

 Minimized downtime during crises and disruptions
 Enhanced resilience of operations, services, and supply chains
 Compliance with regulatory and contractual requirements
 Reduced financial and reputational damage
 Improved stakeholder and customer confidence
 Better risk management and proactive planning
 Faster recovery of critical services and systems
 Competitive advantage in demonstrating preparedness

---

 High Level Structure (HLS)

 Clause 4: Context of the Organization

4.1 Understanding the Organization and Its Context
Identify internal and external issues affecting continuity.

4.2 Understanding the Needs and Expectations of Interested Parties
Stakeholders include:

 Customers and clients
 Regulators and government authorities
 Employees and unions
 Suppliers and partners
 Local communities and society

4.3 Determining the Scope of the BCMS
Define products, services, and processes critical to continuity.

4.4 Business Continuity Management System
Establish, implement, maintain, and improve the BCMS.

---

 Clause 5: Leadership

5.1 Leadership and Commitment
Top management must:

 Take accountability for BCMS effectiveness
 Integrate BCMS into organizational processes
 Provide resources
 Promote continual improvement

5.2 Policy
Business continuity policy must:

 Be appropriate to organization’s purpose
 Commit to continual improvement
 Be communicated and available to interested parties

5.3 Organizational Roles, Responsibilities, and Authorities
Assign roles for risk management, crisis response, communication, and recovery.

---

 Clause 6: Planning

6.1 Actions to Address Risks and Opportunities
Identify risks to continuity and opportunities to strengthen resilience.

6.2 Business Continuity Objectives
Objectives must be measurable, aligned with policy, and regularly reviewed.

6.3 Planning Changes
Ensure planned changes do not negatively affect BCMS.

---

 Clause 7: Support

7.1 Resources – Allocate sufficient financial, human, and technological resources.
7.2 Competence – Ensure employees are trained in business continuity practices.
7.3 Awareness – Employees must understand:

 Their roles during disruptions
 The business continuity policy and procedures
 Potential consequences of not following procedures

7.4 Communication

 Internal – Communication between departments during incidents
 External – Communication with customers, regulators, suppliers, public

7.5 Documented Information – Maintain plans, procedures, and records for continuity and recovery.

---

 Clause 8: Operation

8.1 Operational Planning and Control
Implement processes to achieve BCMS requirements.

8.2 Business Impact Analysis (BIA) and Risk Assessment

 Identify critical activities and processes
 Assess potential impact of disruptions
 Determine maximum acceptable downtime (MADT)
 Evaluate risks and likelihood

8.3 Business Continuity Strategies and Solutions
Define strategies to ensure continuity (redundancy, backup sites, alternative suppliers).

8.4 Business Continuity Plans and Procedures

 Incident response plans
 Recovery plans for IT, facilities, supply chain
 Communication protocols

8.5 Exercising and Testing
Regular testing of plans (e.g., simulation exercises, IT disaster recovery drills).

8.6 Evaluation of Documentation and Capabilities
Review effectiveness of strategies and make improvements.

---

 Clause 9: Performance Evaluation

9.1 Monitoring, Measurement, Analysis, and Evaluation – Measure effectiveness of BCMS.
9.2 Internal Audit – Conduct audits to ensure compliance with ISO 22301.
9.3 Management Review – Top management evaluates suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness.

---

 Clause 10: Improvement

10.1 Nonconformity and Corrective Action – Address incidents and failures, identify root causes, take corrective actions.
10.2 Continual Improvement – Enhance BCMS to ensure ongoing suitability and effectiveness.

---

 Common Disruptive Threats Addressed by ISO 22301

 Natural Threats

 Earthquakes, floods, hurricanes, storms
 Fire, extreme weather, and climate events

 Technological Threats

 IT system failures
 Cyber-attacks and ransomware
 Power outages
 Communication breakdowns

 Human & Organizational Threats

 Strikes, labor disputes
 Insider threats or sabotage
 Key staff unavailability (illness, pandemic)
 Supply chain disruption

 External Threats

 Terrorism and civil unrest
 Political instability
 Regulatory changes
 Economic crises
